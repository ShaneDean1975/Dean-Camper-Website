Types of Truck Accidents We Handle

Our attorneys have extensive experience with all types of commercial vehicle accidents, including:

  • 18-Wheeler Accidents - Tractor-trailer collisions causing catastrophic injuries
  • Semi-Truck Crashes - Multi-vehicle accidents involving large commercial trucks
  • Jackknife Accidents - When a truck's trailer swings out perpendicular to the cab
  • Underride Accidents - Devastating collisions where vehicles slide under trailers
  • Rollover Accidents - Trucks that tip over due to speed, cargo issues, or poor road conditions
  • Wide Turn Accidents - Crashes occurring when trucks make wide right turns
  • Blind Spot Accidents - Collisions in truck "no-zones" where cars aren't visible
  • Rear-End Collisions - Truck driver inattention or brake failure causing rear-end crashes
  • Lost Load Accidents - Injuries from improperly secured cargo falling onto roadways
  • Delivery Truck Accidents - Amazon, FedEx, UPS, and other delivery vehicle crashes

Why Truck Accidents Are Different

Truck accident cases require specialized legal knowledge because they involve:

  • Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations (FMCSR) compliance
  • Commercial driver's license (CDL) requirements and violations
  • Hours of service regulations and logbook analysis
  • Black box data retrieval and interpretation
  • Vehicle maintenance records and inspection reports
  • Cargo securement standards
  • Multiple potentially liable parties (driver, trucking company, maintenance provider, cargo loader, manufacturer)
  • Higher insurance policy limits (often $1 million or more)

Common Causes of Truck Accidents

Through our investigation of hundreds of truck accident cases, we've identified the most common causes of commercial vehicle collisions:

  • Driver Fatigue - Violations of hours of service regulations, pressure to meet delivery deadlines
  • Distracted Driving - Cell phone use, eating, GPS interaction while driving
  • Speeding - Excessive speed for conditions, failure to adjust for truck weight and size
  • Improper Training - Inadequate driver training or lack of experience with large commercial vehicles
  • Poor Maintenance - Brake failures, tire blowouts, mechanical defects
  • Overloaded Trucks - Exceeding weight limits, causing handling and braking problems
  • Improperly Secured Cargo - Shifting loads causing loss of control or falling debris
  • Impaired Driving - Alcohol, drugs, or prescription medication affecting driver performance
  • Aggressive Driving - Road rage, tailgating, unsafe lane changes
  • Weather Conditions - Failure to adjust driving for rain, fog, or other adverse conditions

Serious Injuries in Truck Accidents

The size and weight difference between commercial trucks and passenger vehicles means truck accident victims often suffer severe, life-changing injuries:

  • Traumatic brain injuries (TBI) and concussions
  • Spinal cord injuries and paralysis
  • Neck and back injuries requiring surgery
  • Broken bones and fractures
  • Internal organ damage
  • Severe burns from fires or chemical spills
  • Amputations and loss of limbs
  • Crush injuries
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • Wrongful death

Who Can Be Held Liable?

Truck accident cases often involve multiple liable parties, which is why thorough investigation is critical:

  • Truck Driver - For negligent driving, fatigue, distraction, or impairment
  • Trucking Company - For negligent hiring, inadequate training, or pressuring drivers to violate safety rules
  • Truck Owner - If different from the driver or trucking company
  • Cargo Loading Company - For improper loading or securing of cargo
  • Maintenance Provider - For negligent repairs or failure to maintain the vehicle
  • Parts Manufacturer - For defective truck components or parts
  • Government Entity - For dangerous road conditions or inadequate signage

Federal Regulations Protecting Truck Accident Victims

Our attorneys are thoroughly familiar with FMCSA regulations that trucking companies and drivers must follow:

  • Hours of Service (HOS) limitations - Maximum driving and on-duty time
  • Regular vehicle inspection requirements
  • Driver qualification standards and medical certification
  • Drug and alcohol testing programs
  • Cargo securement standards
  • Electronic logging device (ELD) requirements
  • Maintenance and repair documentation

Violations of these regulations can establish negligence and significantly strengthen your case.

Compensation Available in Truck Accident Cases

Truck accident victims may be entitled to significant compensation for:

  • Medical Expenses - Emergency treatment, hospitalization, surgery, rehabilitation, ongoing care
  • Lost Wages - Income lost due to inability to work during recovery
  • Lost Earning Capacity - Reduced ability to earn income in the future due to permanent disabilities
  • Pain and Suffering - Physical pain and emotional distress caused by injuries
  • Property Damage - Vehicle repair or replacement costs
  • Disability and Disfigurement - Compensation for permanent impairments
  • Loss of Enjoyment of Life - Inability to participate in activities you once enjoyed
  • Wrongful Death Damages - Funeral expenses, loss of companionship, financial support (for fatal accidents)

Time Limits for Truck Accident Claims

In Florida and Alabama, you have a limited time to file a truck accident lawsuit. Florida allows two years from the date of the accident to file most personal injury claims, while Alabama allows two years as well. However, these deadlines can vary depending on the specific circumstances of your case.
