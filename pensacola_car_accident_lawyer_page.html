Compensation You May Be Entitled To

Florida law allows accident victims to recover full compensation for their losses. This includes:

  • Past and future medical expenses (hospital, surgery, physical therapy)
  • Lost wages and reduced earning capacity
  • Pain and suffering
  • Property damage and vehicle replacement
  • Diminished value of your vehicle
  • Out-of-pocket expenses

Florida Car Accident Laws That Affect Your Case

Florida is a no-fault insurance state, meaning your own Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage pays your initial medical bills regardless of who caused the crash. However, when injuries are serious — including significant scarring, permanent injury, or significant loss of bodily function — you have the right to step outside the no-fault system and pursue a claim against the at-fault driver. Florida's modified comparative negligence rule (effective 2023) means your recovery may be reduced if you are found more than 50% at fault.

Florida Statute of Limitations

Florida law gives most car accident victims two years from the date of the accident to file a personal injury lawsuit (F.S. §95.11). Acting quickly is critical to preserving evidence, securing witness statements, and protecting your rights.
